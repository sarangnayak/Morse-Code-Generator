The Language of Dots & Dashes

                
Morse code is a method of transmitting text information as a series of on-off tones, lights, or clicks that can be directly understood by a skilled listener or observer without special equipment.

            

            

                

                

            

        

    


    

        

            

                
Timing is Everything

                
The structure of Morse code is based on precise timing. A "dah" (dash) is three times as long as a "dit" (dot). The space between parts of the same letter is one dit, between letters is three dits, and between words is seven dits.
